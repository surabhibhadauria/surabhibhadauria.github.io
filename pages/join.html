Join the Lab

Why Join SOAR Lab?

Cislunar Space Domain Awareness is emerging as one of the most important frontiers in astrodynamics and space operations. As government and commercial activity rapidly expands throughout the Earth–Moon system, the methods, technologies, and decision frameworks developed today will be fundamental to ensuring safe, secure, and sustainable operations for decades to come.

Our lab sits at the intersection of orbital mechanics, optimization, and multi-sensor systems — a technically rich environment that prepares students for careers at NASA, U.S. Space Force, DoD, and commercial space companies, as well as academic positions.

ERAU's Daytona Beach campus is consistently ranked among the world's top aerospace universities, with strong industry and government connections. Students benefit from a tight-knit aerospace community and regular engagement with practitioners from NASA Kennedy, SpaceX, Lockheed Martin, and others.

What We Look For
🧮

Technical Foundation

Strong math background — linear algebra, differential equations, probability. Experience in MATLAB or Python. Prior coursework in orbital mechanics, astrodynamics, dynamics, or controls is a significant plus.

🔬

Research Drive

Genuine curiosity and self-motivation. Comfort with open-ended problems, reading literature, and iterating when things don't work. We value students who ask deep questions and pursue answers independently.

🤝

Collaboration & Communication

We are a team. Clear written and oral communication, openness to feedback, and willingness to support colleagues are central to our lab culture. We expect students to publish and present their work at top conferences.
