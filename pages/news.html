Grant

FIRST Grant Awarded — Optimized Cislunar Observer Architectures

Dr. Bhadauria is awarded an ERAU Faculty Innovative Research in Science and Technology (FIRST) grant as sole PI. The project, running July 2026 – June 2027, will develop optimized space-based observer architectures for sustained cislunar monitoring.

Three papers accepted at AAS/AIAA Astrodynamics Specialist Conference, Whistler

SOAR Lab members will present at the AAS/AIAA Astrodynamics Specialist Conference in Whistler, BC: constellation design for cislunar surveillance (Quan et al.), multi-sensor data fusion (Bottero, Lovell & Bhadauria), and attitude-orbital dynamics (Abdrabou, Crespo & Bhadauria).

Launch

SOAR Lab established at Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University

Dr. Surabhi Bhadauria joins ERAU as a tenure-track Assistant Professor in Aerospace Engineering and establishes the Space Observation and Astrodynamics Research (SOAR) Lab. The lab focuses on cislunar space domain awareness, observer architecture design, and multi-sensor fusion.
