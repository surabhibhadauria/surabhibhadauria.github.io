Our Research

We develop the mathematical, computational, and observational tools needed to bring persistent awareness to the cislunar domain — the vast region between Earth and the Moon.

Thrust 01

Observer Architecture & Surveillance Trajectory Design

Designing next-generation sensor architectures for persistent Space Domain Awareness in the Earth–Moon system.

As human activity expands beyond Earth orbit, maintaining continuous awareness of objects in cislunar space requires coordinated networks of ground-, space-, and lunar-based sensors. Our research develops optimization and astrodynamics techniques to design resilient surveillance architectures for this emerging operational environment.

  • Design optimal Earth-based optical sensor networks for continuous cislunar monitoring.
  • Develop surveillance satellite constellations using Earth-Moon dynamical orbits.
  • Quantify visibility of key cislunar regions, including Earth-Moon Lagrange points and transfer corridors.
  • Investigate future lunar-based observatories for persistent monitoring of regions inaccessible from Earth.
  • Optimize integrated multi-platform sensor architectures for global cislunar coverage.
Related publications: Bhadauria & Frueh, Adv. Space Research (2026) · Bhadauria, Black & Frueh, J. Astronautical Sciences (2025) · Quan et al., AAS/AIAA (2026) · AMOS (2022, 2024)
Thrust 02

Multi-Sensor Data Fusion for Space Domain Awareness

Combining heterogeneous sensor observations into a unified operational picture of space objects.

No single sensing modality can reliably observe all objects in cislunar space. Our research develops data fusion algorithms that integrate optical, radar, and laser-ranging observations to improve orbit determination, object tracking, and uncertainty quantification.

  • Fuse optical, radar, and laser-ranging observations for enhanced tracking performance.
  • Develop probabilistic methods for uncertainty propagation across heterogeneous sensor data.
  • Improve orbit determination through multi-source measurement integration.
  • Perform sensor registration, coordinate transformation, and time synchronization.
  • Enable robust real-time tracking for future cislunar traffic management.
Related publications: Bottero, Lovell & Bhadauria, AAS/AIAA (2026) · Bhadauria & Frueh, AAS/AIAA (2020)
Thrust 03

Active Space Debris Mitigation & Plasma-Based Technologies

Developing innovative technologies for sustainable and safe utilization of near-Earth space.

The growing population of orbital debris poses significant risks to satellites and future space missions. Our research investigates plasma-based approaches for active debris mitigation and orbital environment management.

  • Investigate plasma-ablation techniques for controlled orbital debris removal.
  • Model plasma–material interactions for space applications.
  • Analyze orbital evolution following active debris mitigation.
  • Support sustainable space operations through advanced mitigation technologies.
Thrust 04

Space Missions, Optical Payloads & Student Flight Experiments

Transforming research into flight opportunities through international collaborations.

Our lab partners with academic and international organizations to develop spaceflight payloads that advance Space Domain Awareness while providing hands-on opportunities for students.

  • Develop an optical payload for Space Domain Awareness aboard the Global Girrlz SAT mission with Space Kidz India.
  • Design compact imaging systems for on-orbit observation and object detection.
  • Develop image processing algorithms for autonomous space-based observations.
  • Involve undergraduate and graduate students in payload design, integration, and testing.
  • Promote international collaboration in space technology and workforce development.
